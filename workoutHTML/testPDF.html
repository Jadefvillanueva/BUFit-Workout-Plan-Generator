General Balanced Workout Plan

  • Monday: Full-Body Strength
    • Warm-Up: Dynamic stretches, light cardio (jumping jacks, jogging).
    • Workout: 3 sets each:
      • Squats – 12 reps
      • Push-ups – 10-15 reps
      • Dumbbell or bodyweight rows – 12 reps per arm
      • Plank – hold for 30-60 seconds
    • Cool-Down: Static stretches (hamstrings, quads, shoulders).
    Watch Full-Body Strength Video
  • Tuesday: Cardio and Core
    • Warm-Up: Light jog or fast-paced walk.
    • Workout:
      • Cardio: Jogging, cycling, or swimming for 20–30 minutes.
      • Core (3 rounds):
        • Russian twists – 20 reps (10 per side)
        • Bicycle crunches – 20 reps
        • Side plank – hold 20–30 seconds per side
    • Cool-Down: Deep breathing and light stretching.
    Watch Cardio and Core Video
  • Wednesday: Active Recovery (Yoga or Light Stretching)
    • Yoga or stretching routine (20–30 minutes).
    • Easy walk or casual sports activity.
    Watch Yoga Flow Video
  • Thursday: Upper Body & Cardio Intervals
    • Warm-Up: Light cardio and arm/shoulder mobility stretches.
    • Workout:
      • Strength (3 sets each):
        • Push-ups – 10-15 reps
        • Dumbbell or bodyweight overhead press – 12 reps
        • Tricep dips – 12-15 reps
      • Cardio Intervals (20 minutes): Alternate 1 minute of fast running/cycling with 2 minutes of walking.
    • Cool-Down: Stretch chest, shoulders, and arms.
    Watch Upper Body & Cardio Video
  • Friday: Lower Body Strength
    • Warm-Up: Same as Monday.
    • Workout (3 sets each):
      • Lunges – 12 reps per leg
      • Deadlifts (with dumbbells or bodyweight) – 12 reps
      • Calf raises – 15 reps
      • Glute bridges – 12–15 reps
    • Cool-Down: Stretch hamstrings, quads, and glutes.
    Watch Lower Body Strength Video
  • Saturday: Cardio & Fun Activity
    • Choose an activity like hiking, biking, or playing sports (30–60 minutes).
    Watch Cardio Inspiration Video
  • Sunday: Rest or Gentle Recovery
    • Light yoga, foam rolling, or stretching (15–20 minutes).
    Watch Recovery Routine Video
